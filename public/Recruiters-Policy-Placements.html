Recruiters Policy for Placements

Structure and Rules for the Placement Process

  1. The proposed final hiring dates allotted to companies for campus placement are arrived at by the IITH committee comprising students, staff and faculty. The dates are allotted based on the information provided in the employer registration form (ERF) submitted by the companies while considering several parameters, including the following not necessarily in any order of priority:

    1. Job profile and career growth prospects

    2. Branch specification given by the company

    3. Likely number of offers this year

    4. Student ratings

    5. Alumni feedback

    6. General research on industry trends

  2. Placement process shall be completed on a slot basis. Each company has been allotted one of the three slots on a particular day. The slot timings are as follows:

    1. Slot 1: 06:00 to 12:00

    2. Slot 2: 13:00 to 19:00

    3. Slot 3: 20:00 to 02:00

  3. Saturdays and Sundays are reserved for conducting tests only. The company may complete their pre-interview tests on a Saturday / Sunday preceding the week in which the hiring happens.
    Each company has been allotted a distinct slot based on the above mentioned criteria. The company is expected to complete their recruitment process within the allotted time (+1 hour of additional time in exceptional cases). Each company must declare final results within the or after completion of their allotted slot time. Failing to do so, the company may lose the students whom it has selected for the final hiring process!

  4. IIT Hyderabad strictly enforces the one-student one-offer policy, with the exception of a single dream-job option. Adhering to the slot structure, and strict timings for declaring the results is necessary to ensure this. Please note that a student is out of the placement process once he/she has an offer in hand.

  5. Companies are hereby requested not to hold the candidates for a long time appearing for the interview, if so, the office may hold the right to take necessary decisions in favor of the student being kept on hold. Company may kindly cooperate with us in this regard.

  6. A copy of the offer letter/Email confirmation must be mailed to the Office of Career Services (office.placement@iith.ac.in) after the results are finalized.

  7. Companies are advised to be in touch with the Student Placement Coordinators and OCS staff at all times. They should not entertain any direct contact from the students appearing in the placement process. In case of such a situation, the company must bring this immediately to the notice of the Placement Office.

  8. In case of an onboarding delay of more than 60 days from their date of coursework, the company has to pay the student the compensation for the delayed period. Extensions to this date will be possible only after approval from the Office of Career Service.

  9. A company that has waitlisted the students is required to release the offer or delete the name from the waitlist within a day so that the student can make a decision.

  10. Recruiters are kindly requested to keep a note of the measures that will be undertaken in case any of the following happens:

    1. Revoking offers stating students are not eligible as per criteria that weren't mentioned earlier in the JD or written communications with the placement team (Background verification excluded)

    2. Companies not adhering to the JD and the terms & conditions (eg: salaries less than filled in ERF, extending the training period and paying less)

    3. Recruiters are not behaving professionally

      • The company will be blacklisted for a certain period.

      • The company will be demoted to a later slot next year.

      • OCS will recommend strict action to be taken across IITs through ALL IIT PLACEMENT COMMITTEE (AIPC)

  11. During the Internship period, recruiters should not terminate the student(s) without a valid reason. Such reasons are for instance:

    1. Poor Performance: 3 times consecutive warning mails (5 days gap) to the interns regarding their poor performance (copy to OCS).

    2. Not Reporting: after 2 warning mails (10 days gap) for not reporting on time to the company

    3. The company should copy to OCS in all such warning mails.


For all matters not covered by the above stated, the Office of Career Services will use its own discretion to make appropriate decisions from time to time and case to case. Moreover, the Institute has full discretion to change/modify the rules from time to time based on the students interest and objective of the OCS.

We look forward to a smooth process and a long-term relationship with the industry.
