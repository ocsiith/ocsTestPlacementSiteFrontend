Internship Rules and Regulations

1. Student Registration

  1. The following are the steps in the Internship process:

    2. Registration with OCS -> company submits IRF -> student coordinator assigned to the company -> registration for company opens -> student registers for the company before deadline -> registration for company closes -> dates for Pre-Placement Talks (PPT) and online/written test announced by coordinator after confirmation of company -> student attends PPT -> student attends tests -> company shortlists the students and informs OCS & student coordinator -> student coordinator announces shortlisting for interviews -> interview slot will be allotted to company -> shortlisted students attend final interview -> Internship offer


  1. The students graduating in the academic year 2024 and seeking Internship in the Internship season 2022-23 should register on OCS portal within the given deadline. Registration through the student portal (OCS) is mandatory.

  2. During the internship process the student has to (i) register in the student portal (one time process) and (ii) register individually with each company for which he/she is eligible and interested. Both these registrations have to be done on OCS portal within the respective deadlines.

  3. Students furnishing false information will be debarred from participating in the internship process.

  4. Registration for internships is a two part process, (i) registration and (ii) creating a student profile. Students must make sure that the entire process is completed on the OCS portal. Partial registrations will not be considered.

2. Master CV

  1. A Master CV is a resume that states absolutely everything that you have done in your career that you might one day include in a resume. By keeping it well formatted, you can copy the document and use it as the base each time you put together a resume to apply for a different position.

  2. The resume with which one applies for a particular company must be a subset of the master resume.

  3. Master resume will not be sent to the company, you need to apply with a different resume for every company. Master resumes once submitted cannot be altered.

  4. The OCS Portal creates your resume automatically in the standard IITH format based on the information provided during registration.

  5. Students must prepare resumes in various formats as per the job profile of individual companies.

  6. These resumes are to be submitted to companies upon request by companies / student coordinators.

  7. The student will be responsible for the accuracy of the information provided in the resume. IITH will not be answerable for any objections about the accuracy of information submitted by students.

  8. The student must safely maintain originals of all certificates (as applicable) including birth, SSC, education, caste, income, medical, domicile, passport, PAN, AADHAR and other certificates etc. These originals should be made available for verification at any time during the process. It is advised to also maintain the above documents as xerox copies and scanned copies.

3. Semester Internship

  1. Students should not have any backlog

  2. Semester Internship is optional

  3. Participants - Only UG pre-final year students

  4. Eligibility - UG Students with CGPA>8.0 at the end of 4th semester

  5. Duration of Internship - 6 months. The semester internship must be of minimum six months duration with a single company. The semester internship can not be fractalized.

  6. Internship Timelines - January to June (i.e. in the 6th semester)

  7. Hiring Timelines - Phase I Fastrack only (July to October i.e. in the 5th semester)

  8. Credits - 6 credits. Evaluation at the end of internship by faculty committee

  9. Internship Report - Students need to submit Internship Report for grading by IITH Faculty

  10. The students should complete the credits of 6th semester missed out due to Semester Internship in any other semesters by end of 8th semester for award of B.Tech degree.

  11. The students will not be allowed to register for any course credits during the semester internship irrespective of whether the internship is onsite or online.

  12. The students should abide by the principle of making use of the semester completely to understand the industry environment and should exceed the expectations of the company offering semester internships. Students should use this opportunity to build professional networks in the industry.

  13. A Student with CGPA>8 can apply for both Summer Internship and Semester Internship in Phase I. However, the first offer received will be the final offer. The student will be considered as “Placed” and will be out of hiring process in any further companies irrespective of

      a) Selection in the type of Internship i.e. Summer / Semester

      b) Students’ acceptance or rejection of offer

  14. IITH follows the “One Student - One Offer” policy.

  15. Non-placed students of Phase-I can apply for Summer Internship in Phase II Rolling during January to April.

  16. Any further updates on Semester Internship will be kept posted on OCS website @ https://ocs.iith.ac.in/

4. Pre Placement Talks (PPT)

  1. It is solely the responsibility of students to check mails / announcements / notices / updates etc. from the Student coordinators / OCS.

  2. Students who have applied for a job opening at a company must attend a Pre-Placement Talk (PPT) conducted by the company. The coordinators will take attendance of the participating students after the start of the session, and a copy will be given to the company for records. In the event that a student misses a PPT, they must fill out the form.

  3. If a student misses more than two PPTs without valid reason (as per OCS policies ), he or she will be disbarred from the placement session.

  4. After attending the PPT the student may decide to de-register for the company soon after the completion of PPT with a valid reason.

  5. It is the sole responsibility of students to check the details / profile of the company before registering or after attending PPT. Students are free to approach the OCS for any queries.

  6. Sometimes, companies may or may not conduct the PPT or may directly conduct the tests (online/offline) without a PPT. In such cases, students should have a clear idea of the company before appearing for the test as de-registration is not allowed after attending the test.


5. Offer of Internship

  1. Once a student receives an offer from any company, he / she is considered as placed irrespective of student’s acceptance or rejection of the offer.

  2. Students receiving an offer from one company will be automatically restricted to apply for any more companies.

  3. Offer letters received by OCS have to be collected by the students.

  4. In case, if the students directly receive the offer letters, the same must be intimated to OCS.

  5. Any mismatch between the job particulars mentioned in the IRF and Offer letter should be brought to the notice of OCS immediately.

  6. Submission of supporting documents to the company before the deadline as per the offer letter is the responsibility of students.

  7. Students who wish to reject an offer should inform the company and OCS immediately with a valid reason.

  8. Blanket rule: Not more than one offer from the Institute. We follow equal opportunity for all registered students, to this effect, students are allowed to have one offer policy.

  9. If you are simultaneously appearing in the internship interviews for two companies say A&B, if the results of A delays and if you are selected in B company, the offer from B company will be treated as the first offer and vice versa.

6. Pre Placement Offer (PPO)

  1. If a student accepts PPO of companies offering packages >20 lakhs (CTC), only the dream option will be available.

  2. If a student accepts a PPO with a package <20 lakhs (CTC), he/she can participate in regular placements limiting to a maximum of five registered companies of their choice.

  3. If a student rejects a PPO, he/she can participate in the regular placements till he/she gets the second offer and will not be eligible for a dream option.

  4. Before rejecting any PPO you are advised to consult with the Office of Career Services.

7. Student Conduct

  1. Attendance of the student in the entire process is compulsory. Unless the student is dropped by the company in one of the steps, the student must attend each step of the internship process as described in (1A)

  2. The student is only allowed to drop out of the process after attending PPT and before writing any tests. Except for this condition, the student is not allowed to drop out of any step in the process.

  3. In case after attending PPT the student decides to drop out then he/she should inform OCS and the respective Student Coordinator.

  4. A valid reason must be provided for dropping out. Approval for de-registration may only be granted if reason found appropriate/valid.

  5. Post closing time no candidate will be allowed in the venue center to appear for APTITUDE TEST/ GD/ INTERVIEW.

  6. Students must be in formal dress code for all interactions with the companies.

  7. OCS holds the complete authority to disapprove a student to attend the selection process, unless attire is satisfactory.

  8. Students must carry their valid IITH identity cards throughout the internship process.

  9. The Student Conduct section of the internship policy is of prime importance. Any sort of misbehavior on the part of students which affects the decorum of the selection process or the reputation of IITH attracts a severe penalty as per the discretion of FIC & DAC. The disciplinary action may be decided in a case-by-case manner and may vary depending on the severity of the situation.
